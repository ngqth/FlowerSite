Carnation Oscar

(Dianthus caryophyllus)

This flower is nothing but the friendliest thing you can have to get into gardening. It has the least maintenance required to grow. What you can do everyday is just water it in the morning, with a cup or just a spray, all will work then leave this guy in the sun. It will grow to be one of the shiniest little fella in your garden. It is the one I choose to help me get a hang of this hobby.

Tip:
Any soil will do, it will live anywhere. Water this little buddy once per month, remember to remove dead flowers or leaves to encorage grow.

↓

Bacopa Triple Colours

(Sutera Cordata)

When you have this flower in your garden, it will look like you have a every flowers ever existed. The reason being, you asked? Because it can grow any color you want. The tip is the ratio between Nitrogen (N), Phosphorous (P) and Potassium (K). Enough with technical chitchat. Imagine you have and RBG color wheel, different ratio between 3 will create a whole new color. What ratio for what color? It will be a little experiment for you to do. Have fun.

Tip:
Plant in a sunny position, remember water it when it gets too hot.

↓

Impatiens Impacio Sun Loving

(Impatien Hawkeri)

This flower is awesome. The flower will grow bunch by bunch. It thrives to show off its colours. Like the name suggest, it loves the sun. So, this will be a perfect choice for someone who live in an apartment with a sunny balcony. The best thing is, the blooming flowers will stay very long.

Tips:
Sun, sun, sun. Remember to grow them in the sun for the best result. Watering can be once per week.

↓

Clove Pink

(Dianthus Caryophyllus)

This is the cousin of Carnation Oscar, the one I mentioned first above. With a wide variety of colours to choose from, you can even grow a rainbow in your backyard. Thing that makes this flower distinctive, it has a lighter scent, perfect for any living room. Similar to its cousin, this flower doesn’t require too much maintenance.

Tips:
Water them once every month. If you plan to use them indoor, choose the most colourful bunch, cut the stem diagonally to increase the contact surface with water, make it stay longer.

↓

Clove Duo Colours

(Dianthus Caryophyllus)

This is another variety of the Clove Pink. It has thinner bush. The flower grows subjectively taller than that of the Clove Pink. Perfect to decorate the background of your yard. One thing to notice, due to its height, I recommend you grow them against any wall or fence. Because it will reduce the wind impact.

Tips:
Grow it where it has less wind, because the flower branch has weaker support than its siblings. Requires a good water during the warmer months.

↓

Cyclamen Mini

(Cyclamen persicum)

If your garden doesn’t have butterflies, it won’t be called a garden, don’t you agree? Worry not, this flower will be there for your rescue. Its street name is literally ‘butterfly flower’, guarantee will bring a new breeze to your scenery. The good thing? These ‘butterflies’ won’t leave your side, ever.

Tips:
Water them with a mist sprayer, don’t use a direct water stream.

↓

Moss Phlox

(Phlox Subulata)

In the movie, they normally show you a hill full of colourful flowers, am I correct? Now you can own that as well. With this kind of specie, they grow very densely at ground level, blanket the whole field. Those blooms normally have a nice soft pink or red colour, but soft-lavender blue is more exclusive.

Tips:
They can grow in any type of soil, wet or dry. Water once every month.

↓

Dahlia

(Dahlia Perennial)

Now I have saved the best for last. This specie has everything that will make your guest say: ‘Hot damn’. Look at the colour, it is basically the sun of your garden. Very reasonable maintained required, but its blooms last more than a fragile rose ever will. The best thing? No spike of course.

Tips:
Water very day using mist sprayer. If use for indoor decorations, lightly spray the bloom to last longer

About the collection

The collection is from years of my experience and research. All the above flowers you can easily get from your local Bunnings stores. Just some recommendations: when picking the best pots for your garden, pretty ones are not normally the best choice. The reason being they have been blooming for a while, by the time you transfer them to soil, the blooms will start to die. The best solution is picking the best-looking plant not flowers, no sign of pests with a lot of un-blossom bulbs.

Good luck building your dream garden. Having fun is the most important thing.
